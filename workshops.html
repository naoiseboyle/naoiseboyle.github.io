Radical Ecologies

Berlin, Friday 28th September

Radical Ecologies is the first in a series of workshops by Radical spaces Laboratory that aims to engage participants to think critically and imaginatively about the composition of our inhabited space and the relationships and interactions contained within. Layers of our ecosystem superimpose, clash and infiltrate one and other producing complex systems of intelligent design and unknown accidents of time and space. How can design engage with this complex space in meaningful ways? How can we experiment with new strategies, systems, technologies, topologies and politics creating new ways of experiencing? What types of tools can we employ to engage with these systems of scale the the environments they produce?

Drawing from methods of speculation, critical thinking and experimentalism we will investigate these layers and co-design responses to complex environments. Participants will explore topologies, discuss and map the different actors that are at play, creating experimental narratives and prototyping artifacts/scenarios that explore new relationships and connections. The workshop structure is focused on co-creation and group dialogue rather than a learning curriculum or lecture and is open for participation to anyone with no necessary experience or background in design. Interested participants can sign up here.

Aims of the Workshop:

To explore the composition of our ecosystems
To engage with critical design methods and new ways of thinking
To co-design and collaborate
Plant ideas for scaffolding further collaboration/thoughts/projects

Workshop Agenda:

Introduction to the Radical Spaces Lab project
Map actors and spaces
Groups generate narratives
Prototype artefacts / systems /scenarios
Discuss created works

Outcomes:

Thinking in new paradigms and creative ways
Co-designing together
Prototyping ideas

Duration:

The workshop will last approximately 3 hours
