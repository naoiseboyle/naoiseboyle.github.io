Towards Radical Design

Radical Spaces Laboratory is a participatory design project that aims to create community based projects on how design can be used for transformative thinking. Participants prototype new cultural experiences that explore the reconceptualisation of normative systems in economic, political, geographic and sociocultural landscapes.

Context

Radical design calls upon design to act as a transformative paradigm providing the conceptual tools to create revolutionary new politics, culture, environments and geographic relationships challenging the status quo and systems of power, space, information and technology. As a culture how can we engage with key issues in society and other complex dynamics in our surrounding ecosystem? How can we reclaim design as a tool of experimentation, transformation, critique and social imagination?

Critical inquiry

Design should employ a critical lens that probes and investigates each layer of the environment deconstructing the space revealing politics, construction, underlying systems and dialogues.

Social action through civic imagination

Design should act as a test bed for radical changes. Thoughts, experiments and ideas flourish into revolutionary ways communities and individuals can change the world around them.

Experimental processes

Design has the power to abstract, conceptualise and rework our understanding of our environment, therefore we work in an experimental space where abstraction and simulation allow for new processes and approaches.

Deconstruction and reimagining of systems

Design should take a radical approach, decolonising legacy power systems, economies, value exchange systems and sociocultural structures, empowering communities to imagine new topologies that maximise freedoms, plurality and new ways of existing.
